Pasta e Fagioli

This soup is the real deal- the traditional broth is thick and creamy, and the flavor from Parmesan rind makes it heavenly. This is a traditional version of the recipe, not the kind you find at Olive Garden. This soup came our phemoninally, defintely one of the best!

I love how colorful the veggies are when they are all chopped up. The whole head of garlic lends a ton o flavor to the soup.

I sauteed everything together until it's nice and soft. As sual, I turn then up to high and stir often until the water cooks off, then turn the heat down to medium and let them finish doing their thing. After that,, you take out 2/3 of the vg and put it into the blender with the beans.

Blend it all up, and throw the pancetta in with the remaining veg.

Once you've got all the inggredients together, add in the rind and bay leaf. After some simmering time, you pull them out.

With a nice parmesan garnish, enjoy the soup with some warm crusty bread. Nothing is better!

Pasta e Fagioli

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 stalks of celery, minced
  • 2 large carrots, minced
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 4 oz pancetta or approx 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 2 cans cannellini beans
  • 1 can great northern beans
  • 1 tsp each of rosemary, thyme, basil, and Italian spices
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 quart chicken broth
  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 1 parmesan rind
  • 1 box ditalini pasta
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parmesan for garnish
  • Crusty bread for serving

Directions

  1. In a large soup pot, heat olive oil. Add onion, celery and carrots and sauté until golden brown, about 10 minutes. Add garlic and cook until brown.
  2. Meanwhile, pour the cans of cannellini beans into a blender or food processor and drain the Great Northern Beans in as well. Add 2/3s of the sautéed veggies. Puree until smooth.
  3. Add the pancetta and herbs to the remaining veggies in the pot. Sauté until pancetta is well browned.
  4. Add the pureed mixture back into the pot, along with the drained beans, the chicken broth, and crushed tomatoes. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add the parmesan rind and bay leaf. Simmer for 30 minutes, remove rind and bay leaf.
  6. While the soup is simmering, cook the pasta according to directions.
  7. Taste soup, and add salt and pepper to taste
  8. Serve, topped with parmesan cheese and crusty bread
