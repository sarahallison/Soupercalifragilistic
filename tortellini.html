Quick Tortellini Tomato Soup

Just because a dish is vegetarian doesn’t mean it isn’t filling! This simple soup makes a fantastic weeknight dinner. Honestly, this soup was done even faster than I thought it would be. AND the leftovers lasted me for 5 meals! This recipe was just full of surprises.

They always say eat the rainbow, and here we see the rainbow! We're gonna chop these peppers up pretty small, the colors always have such a great aestetic. We want them small so they can really cook down until all you're left with is the flavor and nutrition.

We throw these bad boys in with onions and garlic and cook them down. I usually like to cook them up on high until all the water is cooked off, then turn them down to medium and let them cook for a long time until they get a real roasted flavor. After you get all the ingredients together, it has to simmer for a couple of hours for the beans to get nice and soft and for the flavors to meld. Once it's done, I like to blend 2/3s of the soup to make it thick and smooth, but that is toatlly up to you!

Quick Tortellini Tomato Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 Pound Black Beans, Soaked overnight and Rinsed
  • 3 Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 3 Large Sweet Onions, chopped
  • 1 Sweet Red Bell Pepper, chopped
  • 1 Orange Bell Pepper, chopped
  • 1 Green Bell Pepper, chopped
  • 3 Slices of Bacon, chopped
  • 6 Cloves Garlic, Minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons dried oregano
  • 2 tablespoons dried oregano
  • 8 cups Low-Sodium Chicken Broth
  • 1 cup Salsa
  • 2 bay leaves

Directions

  1. Pick through beans for any stones or dirt, rinse well. Put in a large bowl with enough water to fully cover the beans. Soak overnight. Before cooking, drain the beans and rinse thoroughly.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large dutch oven until shimmering. Add in onions and peppers. Start with heat on high until all the water has cooked off from the veggies, then turn it down to medium. Sautee until very brown and soft, at least 15 minutes. This gives them a roasted flavor and really gives the soup a very deep and complex taste.
  3. Add in bacon and cook until crispy. Add in garlic and cook until lightly browned. Add in all the spices and sautee for another minute- toasted spices are much richer.
  4. Add the Beans, Chicken Broth, Salsa, and Bay Leaves. Turn heat back up to high and bring to a boil, stirring often to prevent sticking.
  5. Once it comes to a boil, reduce heat, and cover tightly. Simmer for about 3 hours. Check intermittently and add water whenever needed.
  6. (Optional) Put half the soup into a blender or food processer and blend until smooth. Add back into pot and mix well.
  7. Top with whatever you like! Suggestions are avocado, sour cream, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, jalapenos, and the list goes on. Serve hot and enjoy!
