Matzo Ball Soup

The use of rotisserie chicken and pre-made bone broth makes this a quick and easy recipe for a cold, snowy night. The veggies and seasoning deepen the flavor for an incredible soup! This is a great week-night soup. While it does take some time to simmer, it requires little actiave cooking.

I let the veg cook as I picked the chicken which allowed them to really saute for plenty of time. They developed a very nice flavor in this time- you can rush cooking, but if you allow the food to take its time, it pays off big time.

I got this lovely roast chicken from the supermarket. It was surprisngly juicy and well seasoned! I picked all the meat from it, leaving the carcass in tact. I made sure to save all the juices and add them to the soup. I also threw in the skin which added to the flavor big time.

This was my first time making matzo balls and they came out wonderfully! I just used the recipe on the can of Matzo meal I had. I used my cookie dough scooper to form the matzo balls once they were refrigerated, it worked perfectly! I made this soup right after a big blizzard, so while the matzo balls simmered, I went out and shoveled snow. When I came back in, man was I happy to have this soup!

Matzo Ball Soup

Ingredients

  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 3 medium onions, chopped
  • 2 shallots, chopped
  • 5 carrots, chopped
  • 4 large stalks of celery, chopped
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Herbs and spices to taste
  • A whole rotisserie chicken, with meat picked from the carcass
  • 2 quarts chicken bone broth
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs, slightly beaten
  • 1 cup Matzo Meal
  • 4 tablespoons broth
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions

  1. Sautee onions, shallots, celery, and carrots until well browned. Add garlic, lightly brown. Add whatever herbs and spices you’d like best. I used Bell’s seasoning, which is a mix of powdered herbs for poultry.
  2. Pour in broth and bring to a boil. Put chicken carcass and skin into the pot. Simmer for 20 minutes or longer- the more time those ingredients spend together, the deeper the flavor will become.
  3. During this time, make the matzo balls.
  4. In a bowl beat eggs; add oil, Manischewitz® Matzo Meal and salt. Blend together. Add broth or water and mix until
  5. uniform. Cover and chill in refrigerator for about 20 minutes.
  6. Remove carcass from soup. Make sure to get all bones that may have fallen off. Bring soup back up to a boil.
  7. Moisten hands and
  8. form batter into matzo balls, approximately 1 inch in diameter. Drop matzo balls into pot of boiling broth. Reduce heat , cover tightly and simmer until thoroughly cooked, about 30-40 minutes.
  9. Add in the chicken meat and bring heat to medium until the chicken is warmed.
  10. Top with parsley and serve hot.
